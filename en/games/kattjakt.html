Kattjakt (Discontinued Beta)

Date: 2018-03-16

Screenshots:

This was to be the final in the "Coconuts" trilogy (which I'm assuming familiarity with) that made up most of my early game development history. It was moreover made specifically to be showcased to my middle school class at the time, and it features the class teacher as the main antagonist. Go figure. He was actually a decent guy, the class just wanted him to be the villain. Can't argue with that.

By now, I'd earned some Unity experience, and I think it shows. The camera angle is passable compared to Coconuts on Earth, but it does not do itself any favours by being locked and static. In some sections, you have to go in the opposite direction, and you have no line of sight. That's the least of this game's issues, though. It was never finished, and that was mostly due to overambition clashing with the reality of me not knowing how to actually program or make games.

I had the full story planned out - that reminds me, there are awful voice-acted cutscenes in this game that make me cringe into oblivion - and I had a big expansive world planned, and all that jazz. Now, to give you a small sample of my incompetence, let me ask you something. If you want to implement a way to backtrack in your game, how would you do it? How would you make the game know where to spawn you in the scene if you can enter the scene from multiple directions? Maybe you'd have a static variable that tracks where to place you upon entering the scene? Maybe you wouldn't have a separate gameobject for each scene, and instead just edit your player's position on the fly to make it go where you want it to go?

My genius solution was having upwards of 5 copies of each level for backtracking purposes. Yes. I'm serious. One for when you go through it, one for if you go back to it from the next level, one for if you go back to it from the next level after already going back to the next level from the one after that. Ad infinitum.

It should surprise no one that this game was never finished.

Coconuts on Earth

Date: 2017-11-10

Screenshots:

After the relative success of Coconuts in Space (it compiled), a sequel was the obvious next progression. Well, for me at the time, at least. Nowadays, the natural progression would likely be dumping the whole project into the trash. Alas, I was still 12. I decided that the next version should have the camera following the player, something I tried accomplishing in Coconuts in Space but couldn't figure out. While I succeeded, my implementation was subpar when combined with the other elements of the game.

It was an interesting design choice to use a Terrain in the initial levels of the game. It's a very open environment, which is typically not what you want for a game like this. The common experience I saw with most people unfortunate enough to try the game was getting lost. The angle combined with the open level design meant a lot of those levels is just spent wandering around. The enemies pose no threat, it's just painfully boring. I think I recognised this even back then, because the later levels are more linear and are in closed quarters.

This is where the game stops being miserably bad and just starts being bad. That's not saying much. These levels are also very short. The final boss stage is also downright broken. On low-end hardware, player movement freezes every time the lights flash. I don't know why. This doesn't happen on my testing machines. You're not missing out on much - just another crappy cutscene.

Sigh. This game is an insult.
